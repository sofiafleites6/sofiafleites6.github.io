The Greek Government

Government type

parliamentary republic

Capital

Athens is the oldest European capital city; according to tradition, the city is named after Athena, the Greek goddess of wisdom; in actualiyu, the appellation probably derives from a lost name in a pre-Hellenic language.

Constitution

The latest revision of the Constitution was done in June of 1975. Amendments must be proposed by at least 50 members of Parliament and agreed by three-fifths majority vote in two separate ballots at least 30 days apart; passage requires absolute majority vote by the next elected Parliament; entry into force finalized through a "spcial parliamentary resolution"; articles on human rights and freedoms and the form of government cannot be amended. Amendments have been made in 1986, 2001, 2008, and 2019.
